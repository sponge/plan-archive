<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">As
per the readme.txt in the Daikatana Demo:&nbsp; My god, it's finally
finished.&nbsp; And I thought working on a game for 1.5 years was
long....wow.&nbsp; I wish everyone would take a nice, objective look at the game
and not base their criticisms on hype, but on play value and what we've worked
to achieve: a really fun single-player experience.&nbsp; We did not develop
Daikatana to take on Quake 3 or UT, but we put multiplayer in the game because I
really like my single-player games multiplayerable.&nbsp; Is that a new
word?&nbsp; :)</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">&nbsp;There
have been a few technical issues with the demo that you can fix or workaround
with the following information:</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(Q1)
Multiplayer is laggy or extremely choppy.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(A1)
The server should have its <b>CONNECT RATE </b>turned up to <b>T1/LAN </b>(in
the Multiplayer Menu).&nbsp; Make sure your client connect rate is turned up as
well.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(Q2)
Daikatana doesn't work with a <b>Voodoo2</b>.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(A2)
There are two fixes for this: (1) edit your config file (in the DATA
subdirectory) and change &quot;<b>opengl32</b>&quot; to <b>&quot;3dfxgl&quot; or
&quot;3dfxvgl&quot;</b>, (2) download and install the <b>miniGL driver </b>from&nbsp;
<a href="http://www.3dfxgamers.com/view.asp?PAGE=V2drivers">http://www.3dfxgamers.com/view.asp?PAGE=V2drivers</a></p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(Q3)
Running the <b>DKDEMO.EXE </b>on <b>Windows 2000 </b>gives you an error (Insert
Disk 1)</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(A3)
Use WINZIP to extract the <b>DKDEMO.EXE </b>file, then run the <b>SETUP.EXE</b>
program to install the demo</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(Q4)
The game pauses for a long time when you're playing multiplayer (seems like a
lockup)</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(A4)
This is a bug in the <b>demo only </b>that occurs when you are attempting to
connect to the <b>Catacomb </b>level (Greek time period).&nbsp; Just wait 20
seconds and you'll connect.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(Q5)
The game is too dark</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">(A5)
Check out the README.TXT file, it has some useful tips.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">So
far, these cover 99% of the problems that people are having.&nbsp; I'd like to
say thanks to everyone who has been emailing me their raves and congrats --

<h6 style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; background-color: rgb(0,0,128); color: rgb(255,255,255); border-left: 10px none rgb(0,0,128); border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding-left: 0; padding-right: 0; padding-top: 5; padding-bottom: 05" align="left">&nbsp;&nbsp;
December 10, 1999</h6>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">It
was six short years ago, down to the hour.&nbsp; I remember staying up the last
30 hours doing exhaustive testing before Jay Wilbur uploaded the shareware
version of DOOM to the University of Wisconsin's FTP site.&nbsp; We couldn't get
in -- too many people were sitting on the site, waiting for our upload.&nbsp;
The target directory was full of bogus files with names like &quot;when.is.it.coming&quot;
and &quot;hurry.up.id&quot;, but finally we got the sysop to up the connect
limit to 250 and we barely got in.&nbsp; After that upload, things changed.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0" align="left">I
remember the crazy lockup bug we had the day before -- if DOOM sat running for
too long, it would just lock up.&nbsp; Turned out to be a value that was being
incremented by the 140-tic-per-second timer function that was wrapping around
(it takes a very long time for a LONG to wrap, even at
140-tics-per-second,&nbsp; but this one was uninitialized so it took even less
time).&nbsp; So, we finished it up and sent it out, just knowing everyone would
have a lot of fun with it.&nbsp; Happy Birthday, DOOM.</p>
