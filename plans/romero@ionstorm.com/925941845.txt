<p

align="left">Happy Birthday Wolfie!&nbsp; Seven long years ago, the shareware version of
Wolfenstein 3-D was uploaded to Software Creations BBS.&nbsp; It was very late at night
(in fact, i think it was 5am) and all our exhaustive testing was done, so we uploaded the
final ZIP file and crashed.&nbsp; Scott Miller and Bobby Prince were there for the
shareware upload since they were just as excited about Wolfie as everyone else.&nbsp; </p>

<p
style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0"
align="left">We were running id out of a 1-bedroom loft apartment in Mesquite (La Prada
Club) and the company had a grand total of 6 employees: Tom Hall, John Carmack, Adrian
Carmack, Jay Wilbur, Kevin Cloud and myself.&nbsp;&nbsp; The next couple months were spent
finishing the last 3 episodes (6 in total) and Tom, Kevin and I wrote the Wolfenstein 3-D
Hint Book using NeXT hardware for layout.&nbsp; That was the first and only hint book id
would ever write.&nbsp; Things got *very* busy after that point...</p>

<p
style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0"
align="left">Carmack is totally right about moving as much as possible over to the client
so as to reduce as much packet traffic as possible.&nbsp; We're doing this right now in
Daikatana by moving lots of animations over to the client so packet traffic is low.&nbsp; </p>

<p
style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12; border-left: 10px none; border-top: 2px; border-bottom: 2px; padding: 0"
align="left">For example, our Shockwave weapon (a steel ball that blasts out, hits a wall,
then explodes with tremendous force in an ever-expanding sphere of death and destruction)
has all the animation on the client side, even the expanding, rotating&nbsp; rings of
death and all the hit-checking is done on the server with no packets being sent out saying
which frames need to be drawn.&nbsp; With this BFG-style weapon, that's a good thing
because there are about 8 massive objects being drawn all at once!</p>
